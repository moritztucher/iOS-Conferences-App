## Layer — glossy gift-card ticket

A square 1024×1024 PNG with a fully transparent background.

One event ticket of glossy warm-white polycarbonate (#F6F4EF, the "iBook white" acrylic of the original iPod's face): a horizontal rounded rectangle, 640×280 pixels, 36-pixel corner radius, centered at (512, 512), rotated 8 degrees with the right end raised. One soft diagonal specular band with the faintest Aqua-blue tint (#1A6DD2 at very low opacity) sweeps across the upper half — glossy plastic, not paper — and a faint cool highlight along the top edge so the card reads slightly thick.

The right 192 pixels are a tear-off stub, separated by a vertical perforation seam: a dashed line in cool light gray (#C6C8CA, 3 pixels thick) running between two semicircular notches of 22-pixel radius punched into the top and bottom edges at the seam — the notches are cut through, showing the transparent background.

All printing is near-black ink (#1C1C1E), crisp like screen-printed plastic, rotated with the ticket:
1. Left, inset 48 pixels: a center-aligned two-line stack in slender Apple-Garamond-style serif capitals, 34 pixels tall per line — "1,000 SONGS" / "IN YOUR POCKET" — vertically centered on the same horizontal line as the "2001" numerals.
2. To its right, centered in the remaining width, a center-aligned stack: "OCT 23" in light humanist sans-serif capitals, 42 pixels tall; "2001" in semibold humanist numerals, 110 pixels tall, the focal point; "APPLE CAMPUS, CUPERTINO" in small capitals, 22 pixels tall.
3. On the stub, horizontally and vertically centered: a classic white earbud with one gracefully looping cable, drawn as a clean line illustration in the same ink, about 115 pixels tall.

Nothing else — no rules, no barcode, no shadows onto the background, nothing touching the canvas edges. Verify: "1,000 SONGS", "IN YOUR POCKET", "OCT 23", "2001", "APPLE CAMPUS, CUPERTINO".
