## Layer — glossy keynote ticket

A square 1024×1024 PNG with a fully transparent background.

One event ticket of glossy near-black glass (#0A0A0C, the face of the original iPhone): a horizontal rounded rectangle, 640×280 pixels, 36-pixel corner radius, centered at (512, 512), rotated 8 degrees with the right end raised. A thin polished chrome edge (#C7CBD0, about 4 pixels) runs around the entire silhouette like the original's steel bezel. One glossy Aqua-style highlight arc tinted pale cyan-white (#BFE3F2) sweeps across the upper half — the "lickable" gloss of iPhone OS 1 — brightest at the top, fading before the vertical middle.

The right 192 pixels are a tear-off stub, separated by a vertical perforation seam: a dashed line in medium cool gray (#585E64, 3 pixels thick) running between two semicircular notches of 22-pixel radius punched into the top and bottom edges at the seam — the notches are cut through, showing the transparent background.

All printing is bright near-white ink (#F5F6F7) in clean Myriad-style type with a faint glass glint, rotated with the ticket:
1. Left, inset 48 pixels: a center-aligned three-line quote in mixed case, 30 pixels tall per line — "Today, Apple is" / "going to reinvent" / "the phone." — vertically centered on the same horizontal line as the "2007" numerals.
2. To its right, centered in the remaining width, a center-aligned stack: "JAN 9" in capitals, 42 pixels tall; "2007" in semibold numerals, 110 pixels tall, the focal point; "MOSCONE CENTER, SAN FRANCISCO" in small capitals, 20 pixels tall.
3. On the stub, centered exactly both horizontally and vertically — equal space above and below, never shifted toward the bottom: the original 2007 iPhone drawn as an illustration, a vertical rounded slab with chrome outline, dark screen, and home button, about 115 pixels tall.

Nothing else — no rules, no barcode, no shadows onto the background, nothing touching the canvas edges. Verify: "Today, Apple is", "going to reinvent", "the phone.", "JAN 9", "2007", "MOSCONE CENTER, SAN FRANCISCO".
