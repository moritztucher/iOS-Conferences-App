## Layer — letterpress ticket

A square 1024×1024 PNG with a fully transparent background.

One event ticket of aged ivory letterpress paper (#F2E8D5, like the 1976 Apple-1 manual's cover stock): a horizontal rounded rectangle, 640×280 pixels, 36-pixel corner radius, centered at (512, 512), rotated 8 degrees with the right end raised. Subtle grain, a few faint foxing specks, slightly soft deckled edges (the silhouette stays clean), faint highlight along the top edge so the paper reads slightly thick.

The right 192 pixels are a tear-off stub, separated by a vertical perforation seam: a dashed line in aged sepia (#8B5A2B, 3 pixels thick) running between two semicircular notches of 22-pixel radius punched into the top and bottom edges at the seam — the notches are cut through, showing the transparent background.

All printing is letterpress in India-ink near-black (#1E1A15), pressed slightly into the paper with subtly uneven coverage, rotated with the ticket:
1. Left, inset 48 pixels: a woodcut-style engraving of Isaac Newton sitting and reading under an apple tree, about 180×150 pixels, fine old-book-plate line work, vertically centered on the same horizontal line as the "1976" numerals.
2. To its right, centered in the remaining width, a center-aligned stack: "APRIL 1" in letterpress serif capitals, 44 pixels tall; "1976" in very heavy serif numerals, 120 pixels tall, the focal point; "LOS ALTOS, CALIFORNIA" in small serif capitals, 24 pixels tall.
3. On the stub, horizontally and vertically centered: an apple with a single leaf, drawn as a woodcut-style line illustration in the same ink, about 110 pixels tall.

Nothing else — no rules, no barcode, no shadows onto the background, nothing touching the canvas edges. Verify: "APRIL 1", "1976", "LOS ALTOS, CALIFORNIA".
