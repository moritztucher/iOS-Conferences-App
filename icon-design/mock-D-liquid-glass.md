# Mock D — Liquid-Glass Ticket (WWDC26 design language)

The strongest direction: the ticket rendered in Apple's **WWDC26 "Liquid Glass /
liquid-chrome"** language — a polished, translucent molten-glass object on pure black,
with smooth flowing highlights, prismatic edge refraction (warm amber caustic + cool
blue dispersion), and a soft outer bloom. This is also the app's own stated design
language (iOS 26 Liquid Glass), so the icon and the app speak the same visual dialect.

Reference: WWDC26 chrome Apple-logo artwork — glossy liquid-metal surface, black
backdrop, orange/blue chromatic light sweeping across the form, gentle glow.

Shared design (constant across all four):
- **Pure-black background** with a soft radial bloom behind the object.
- A **single event ticket** rendered as **polished translucent liquid glass / liquid
  chrome** — highly reflective, smooth flowing specular highlights, slight 3D depth,
  a perforated stub edge (holes rimmed in light).
- **Prismatic refraction** along the curves and edges: warm amber-gold on one sweep,
  cool blue-violet dispersion on another (chromatic aberration at the rims).
- The **date is etched / embossed into the glass surface**, bold sans-serif, catching
  the light — legible, the focal point.

Per variant, change the **date** and which colour the refraction **leans**:

| Icon (default first) | Date          | Refraction lean              | Meaning                        |
|----------------------|---------------|------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| **Main**             | `1 APR 1976`  | warm amber-gold + faint blue | Apple founded                  |
| iPhone               | `9 JAN 2007`  | cool silver-blue             | Original iPhone keynote        |
| Macintosh            | `24 JAN 1984` | green-teal + silver          | First Macintosh                |
| Launch / WWDC26      | `8 JUN 2026`  | **exact WWDC26 orange+blue** | WWDC26 & release day           |

**Output format:** flat **square** 1024×1024, no rounded corners (iOS masks at display
time), no shadow beyond the canvas edge. GPT Image — verify the etched date renders
exactly; reply "fix the text to read exactly: …" if garbled. Etched-on-glass text is
the riskiest part — if it fails, fall back to the date on a frosted inset panel.

## 1 — Main · 1 APR 1976 (amber-gold) ★ default

soft radial bloom of light behind the centre. A single event ticket floating centred,
rendered as a polished translucent liquid-glass / liquid-chrome object — highly

glow/bloom around the whole form. Premium, cinematic, Apple "Liquid Glass" aesthetic.
Square image 1024×1024, full-bleed, square corners, no text outside the artwork.
