# Mock A — Easter-Egg Era Tickets (blueprint base)

Alternate-app-icon set built on the **mock-A blueprint** base (`mock-A-tickets.md`).
The **blueprint-blue draftsman background is constant** across all four icons; only the
**ticket** changes. Each ticket is a **period-accurate artifact** of its moment — drawn
in the graphic language of that era — and is stamped with the date and the **venue** that
matters to that event. The concept: historic Apple milestones pinned to a blueprint.

One prominent era-ticket per icon (so the period styling reads clearly), slightly tilted,
with a perforated stub, sitting on the shared blueprint with a soft ground shadow.

| Icon (default first) | Date          | Venue                                   | Era design language                              |
|----------------------|---------------|-----------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| **Main**             | `APR 1, 1976` | Los Altos, California                   | 1976 woodcut Newton logo, sepia engraving, serif |
| iPhone               | `JAN 9, 2007` | Moscone Center, San Francisco           | 2007 glossy Aqua glass + brushed aluminium       |
| Macintosh            | `JAN 24, 1984`| Flint Center, Cupertino                 | 1984 six-colour rainbow logo, Chicago bitmap     |
| Launch / WWDC26      | `JUN 8, 2026` | Apple Park, Cupertino                   | WWDC26 Liquid Glass chrome, orange/blue refraction|

**Shared background (identical in every prompt):** Apple "blueprint" style — bright blue
gradient from sky-blue top-left (#5FA8FF) through mid-blue (#2C7BEE) to deep blue
bottom-right (#1457C8), overlaid with a faint white draftsman's grid (fine 64px + stronger
256px, ~15–20% opacity), subtle white concentric guide circles centred on the canvas,
horizontal/vertical centre lines, and small white corner-registration ticks — all low
opacity. A soft elliptical ground shadow beneath the ticket.

**Output format:** flat **square** 1024×1024, no rounded corners (iOS masks at display
time), no shadow beyond the canvas edge. GPT Image — verify the date and venue render
exactly; reply "fix the text to read exactly: …" if garbled.
